About

From the storied workrooms of Savile Row, Finn's career has been shaped by the highest standards of both Savile Row heritage and modern design. Mentored by some of Savile Row's most respected artisans, he honed his skill through years of dedication to precision, fit, and form. Finn is redifining bespoke tailoring for a new generation - bridging timeless craftsmanship with a distinctly contemporary eye.

Process

Consultation & Design Discussion

Each commission begins with a personal conversation to understand your style, preferences, and the occasions for which the garment will be worn. Together, we explore fabric choices, design details, and the overall aesthetic to guide you to the first fitting.

Measurement & Assessment

I take 17 measurements to use in the drafting process. Every aspect of your posture, stance, and movement is carefully observed and measured. These notes ensure that the finished garment complements your natural form and comfort, not just your dimensions.

Drafting & Cutting

A paper pattern is then drawn entirely by hand. Drafted precisely to your body's dimensions. Once finished, your pattern is laid out onto the fabric of your choice and cut with meticulous attention to grain, alignment, and balance.

First Fitting (Baste Stage)

The initial fitting is hand stitched in-house ahead of your first fitting. This allows for the fine-tuning of pattern alterations for balance, drape, and proportion before your garment is finalized. After the first fitting, your garment will be ripped down to its two-dimenstional form where I will incorporate any adjustment necessary to your pattern before moving ahead to your second and final fitting.

Second Fitting

Once all the aspects of balance and fit are addressed in the baste fit stage, your garment will then be sent out to my network of expert Savile Row trained makers for finishing. Each garment made from years of experience and shared knowledge, passed down through hundreds of years of English tailoring tradition.

Hand Finishing & Final Presentation

Every detail is completed by hand - from buttonholes and pressing to final shaping. Once inspected and perfected, the finished garment is presented, and your pattern is kept for future commissions.
